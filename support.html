How do I give the app permission to access my calendar?
When you first try to save an event, iOS will ask for calendar permission. Tap "Allow" to grant access. If you accidentally denied permission:
  1. Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Calendars
  2. Find "Text to Calendar" in the list
  3. Toggle the switch to enable access
  4. Restart the app
Why isn't my text being parsed correctly?
The AI parser works best with clear, natural language. Try these tips:
  • Include specific dates: "tomorrow at 2pm" instead of "later"
  • Use clear time formats: "2:00 PM" or "2pm"
  • Include location with "at" or "@": "at Starbucks" or "@ my office"
  • Try the sample examples in the app first
Can I create recurring events?
Yes! The app supports recurring events in two ways:
  1. Automatic detection: Use phrases like "every Friday" or "daily meeting"
  2. Manual selection: After parsing, tap to edit and select recurrence options
Supported patterns: Daily, Weekly, Bi-weekly, Monthly, Yearly
Where do my events get saved?
Events are saved directly to your iOS Calendar app. They appear in:
  • Your default calendar (usually "Personal")
  • The Calendar app on your iPhone/iPad
  • Any connected calendars (iCloud, Google, etc.)
How do I view or delete events I've created?
  1. Tap the calendar icon in the top-right corner of the app
  2. View all events created by the app
  3. Swipe left on any event to delete it
Note: Deleted events are permanently removed from your calendar
Is my data private and secure?
Absolutely! Text to Calendar is privacy-first:
  • All text processing happens on your device
  • No data is sent to external servers
  • We don't collect or store your personal information
  • Only calendar access is requested (and only when you save events)
Why do I see ads in the app?
Text to Calendar is free to use and supported by advertisements. The ads help us keep the app free while maintaining privacy. We use Google AdMob for ad delivery, which follows industry privacy standards.
What devices and iOS versions are supported?
  • iPhone: iOS 17.0 and later
  • iPad: iPadOS 17.0 and later
  • Compatibility: All iPhone and iPad models that support iOS 17+

üîß Troubleshooting

Calendar Permission Issues

Problem: App says "Calendar access denied" or events don't appear
Solution:
  1. Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Calendars
  2. Find "Text to Calendar" and enable access
  3. Restart the app
  4. Try creating an event again

Parsing Not Working

Problem: Text isn't being parsed into event details
Solution:
  1. Try the sample examples in the app first
  2. Use more specific language: "Meeting tomorrow at 2:00 PM"
  3. Include clear dates and times
  4. Check that your text contains recognizable patterns

Events Not Appearing in Calendar

Problem: Events are saved but don't show up in Calendar app
Solution:
  1. Check that your default calendar is set in iOS Settings
  2. Look in the Calendar app for newly created events
  3. Verify the date/time of the event
  4. Check if you have multiple calendars enabled

üí° Tips for Better Results

Best Practices for Text Input

  • Be specific: "Lunch with Sarah tomorrow at 1:00 PM" vs "lunch tomorrow"
  • Use clear formats: "2:00 PM" or "2pm" work better than "2"
  • Include locations: "at Starbucks" or "@ my office"
  • Add context: "Team meeting" or "Doctor appointment"
  • Specify duration: "for 1 hour" or "until 5pm"

Example Phrases That Work Well

  • Meeting with John tomorrow at 2pm at the office
  • Doctor appointment on Dec 15 at 10:00 AM for 1 hour
  • Team sync next Monday 9am-10am in Conference Room A
  • Call with Priya in 2 hours - remind 30 minutes before
  • Birthday party on 2024-12-25 6:30 PM at my place
